# Brand Voice & Tone Guide

## Who We're Talking To

Tech founders and early SaaS operators. Seed to Series A. Mostly US West Coast and Canada. They can code, they've shipped a product, and they've read every Paul Graham essay. They think that makes them smarter than everyone else in the room. It doesn't. Classic Dunning-Kruger.

They're not stupid. They're just overconfident about things outside their expertise, and marketing is one of them. They've tried posting on LinkedIn. It went nowhere. They've thought about hiring someone. They haven't. They're convinced they could figure it out themselves if they just had more time.

They won't.

## The Core Tension

We speak simply because simple works. But we frame everything so they feel smart for understanding it. Think Apple: dead simple products, marketed to make you feel like a genius for owning one.

Treat them like a bright 15-year-old who knows what an API is. Don't talk down. Don't talk up. Just be clear, be direct, and let them feel like they're the ones who "got it."

## Vocabulary They Respond To

Use these naturally, not as a list:
- MRR, ARR, CAC, LTV, churn
- Pipeline, ICP, GTM, PMF
- Founder mode, vibe coding
- Signal, noise, north star, feedback loop
- Stack, layer, system, loop
- Organic, reach, impressions (but only when it matters)

Drop them the way a person drops them in conversation. Not as jargon. As shorthand between people who know what they're talking about.

## What We Sound Like

- Direct. One idea per sentence.
- Confident without performing confidence.
- Slightly knowing, never condescending.
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs.
- We say the obvious thing clearly instead of dressing it up.
- We don't explain what we're about to say before we say it.

## What We Don't Sound Like

- Corporate: "We leverage data-driven strategies to unlock growth."
- Motivational: "We help brands reach their full potential."
- Vague: "We tell your story."
- Branding-workshop: "We find your soul, your essence, your why."
- AI slop: anything with "tapestry," "landscape," "robust," "seamless," or "transformative."

## Tone in Practice

**Bad:** "We leverage human-centric marketing strategies to drive meaningful engagement across digital touchpoints."

**Good:** "You're spending on content and influencers. But when you pull the metrics, the investment doesn't justify the return. If there's even any return. You have a better product. You probably have more resources. Yet your competitor is getting all the engagement. We fix that."

---

**Bad:** "In today's fast-paced digital world, authentic connection is more important than ever."

**Good:** "People can tell when something was written by a bot. Your buyers can too."

---

**Bad:** "We are a full-service boutique agency specializing in B2B and DTC marketing solutions."

**Good:** "We're a handful of people who do influencer marketing, content, and social really well. We work with SaaS startups and DTC brands. That's it."

## The Damaging Admission

We don't run paid ads. We don't make videos. We don't have a team of 40 people managing your account. What we do, we do well. If you need something we don't offer, we'll tell you that upfront, and you'll trust us more for it.

## The Closing Offer (how we bring in new clients)

Before any retainer, we work together for 30 days. $1,500 to $2,000. At the end of the month, either we keep going or we don't. No pressure, no drama. This is how most of our client relationships start.
