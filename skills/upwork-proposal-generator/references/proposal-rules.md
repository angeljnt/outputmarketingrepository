# Upwork Proposal Rules

This file defines the frozen proposal logic. The structure should stay stable. The knowledge base supplies the job-specific substance.

## Target audience

### ICP

- Dunning-Kruger demographic: people who think they are experts but usually operate with surface-level knowledge.

### Communication strategy

- Talk simply and clearly.
- Use strategic words like `strategic`, `systems`, `framework`, `optimization`, `conversion`, and `funnel` without sounding jargon-heavy.
- Make complex things sound simple while still signaling competence.
- Let them feel smart for understanding you.
- Never talk down to them.

## Standard full proposal structure

Use these seven sections in this order.

### 1. Opening hook

Format:
- `**Hey [Name]!**` with a wave emoji
- one sentence that promises outcome plus protection from a specific risk

Rules:
- concise
- personalized
- directly tied to the client's main challenge
- outcome plus protection

### 2. Value-focused body

Use client-centered language such as:
- `Here's what you'll get when we work together: ...`
- `You'll get ...`
- `Your [business aspect] will finally ...`

Rules:
- benefits over biography
- outcomes over features
- no `resonates with me`
- no generic AI filler

### 3. Strategic proof points

Only use proof that matters for this exact job.

Primary sources available in the KB:
- Webby Award
- Archive.com experience
- InterruptMedia experience
- Apollo.io experience
- StudySmarter experience
- agency ownership
- budget management
- portfolio

Rules:
- no credential dumping
- include only relevant proof
- use full URLs without hyperlinking in body text
- exception: portfolio and Webby Award can be hyperlinked if helpful
- prefer `content` over `campaigns` when that wording is more accurate
- avoid specific engagement percentages or invented metrics
- prefer phrases like `100 per cent consistency in publication schedules` and `sustained growth`

### 4. Unique differentiators

Common differentiator families:
- AI-enhanced marketing
- no-code tools
- agency ownership mindset
- cross-industry experience
- full-funnel thinking

Rules:
- choose only what strengthens this job
- write in plain language
- keep the tone sharp, useful, and grounded
- when using the AI-enhanced angle, keep it operational and concrete rather than futuristic
- one strong paragraph beats three weak AI claims

### 5. What makes this work for you

Use the exact label:
- `What makes this work for you:`

Include only relevant work-style strengths such as:
- reliable
- proactive
- asks good questions early
- flags issues early
- adapts when priorities change
- comfortable in startups
- brings solutions, not problems
- does not need micromanaging

Tone:
- confident
- direct
- not arrogant

### 6. Simple call-to-action

Format:
- `My next step would be a quick call to discuss [their challenge/goals] and show you exactly how I'll [specific outcome].`

Rules:
- one clear next step
- no elaborate process
- conversation, not hard sell

### 7. Closing question

Default format:
- `**By the way! Could you share your website so I can better understand [their brand/audience/solution]?**`

Allowed variations:
- website
- Instagram handles
- biggest challenge
- one specific business area

Rules:
- one question only
- easy to answer
- natural and diplomatic
- `Would you mind sharing your website?` is an acceptable softer variation when the overall proposal is already confident

## Writing style rules

### Tone

- personable
- natural
- impactful
- approachable with a little edge

### Preferred language

- `I know how to...`
- `I've done this for...`
- `Here's what makes this work...`
- `The thing is...`
- `What makes me right for this...`

### Avoid

- `mastering`
- `dominating`
- `resonates with me`
- `excited to`
- `passionate about` unless it is truly the right phrase
- `stands at a`
- obvious AI prose
- mirrored `as X, as Y` statements

## Metrics and numbers

Avoid:
- made-up statistics
- invented percentages
- inflated counts

Use only:
- verifiable facts
- `$500k+ budget management`
- `100 per cent consistency in publication schedules`
- `sustained growth`

## Accuracy rules

- CaseEasy = Immigration LegalTech
- ZenGRC = RegTech for GRC compliance
- Vanessa = digital marketing strategist across industries, not a one-sector specialist
- timeline = agency -> Archive.com -> rebuilding agency
- no fake achievements

## Proposal variations

### Short proposal

- same logic, condensed
- three to four paragraphs max
- keep hook, value, CTA, and question

### Message response

- more conversational
- responds to the message directly
- can compress sections heavily

### Complex or unique jobs

- deeper demonstration that you understand the brief
- longer proof section if needed
- still keep the skeleton intact

## Job-type emphasis

- Social media management: Archive CEO social media, Webby Award, consistency
- B2B SaaS marketing: Archive.com, InterruptMedia, Apollo.io, budget management, full-funnel thinking
- Content creation or writing: InterruptMedia, Webby Award, content framing
- Content operations or execution-heavy marketing roles: Archive.com, cross-channel delivery, HubSpot, WordPress, workflows, independent execution
- EdTech or education: StudySmarter
- Agency or freelance management: agency ownership, one-woman-army experience, systems thinking
- No-code or tech tools: AI-enhanced marketing, no-code tools
- Performance-based roles: comfort with retainer plus bonus or other variable compensation

## Pre-send checklist

- Name is correct and personalized.
- Tone is natural and conversational.
- Focus stays on their benefit, not Vanessa's biography.
- Only relevant proof points are included.
- No `mastering`, `dominating`, `resonates with me`, or `excited to`.
- No fake numbers or fake percentages.
- URLs are formatted correctly.
- Bold formatting is used strategically.
- Company descriptions are accurate.
- One simple closing question is included.
- The proposal reads simply but still signals sophistication.
