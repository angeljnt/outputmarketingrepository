# SOTA Report: Output Marketing — Product Offering & Landing Page Structure
Date: 2026-03-22
Research scope: Boutique B2B SaaS & DTC marketing agency — service packaging, pricing architecture, and website structure

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **3 productized tiers** is the validated structure. Not 2, not 6. Starter / Core / Premium — with a named pilot offer below all three.
- **Outcome-led packaging** converts. "855K impressions for Airwallex" beats "12 posts/month."
- **Agency websites need 7 core pages** minimum. Case studies are non-negotiable — 77% of B2B buyers rely on them before buying.
- **Single CTA per section.** Multiple CTAs cut conversions up to 266%.
- **Founder-forward About page** converts faster than team pages for boutique agencies.
- **Public pricing builds trust** and pre-qualifies bad-fit clients before they waste your time.
- **Pilot offer is the hook.** Every expert and data point confirms: low-risk entry = faster yes = higher retention.

## 2. Product Offering Structure

### The 4-Tier Model (Pilot + 3 Retainers)

```
PILOT (Entry Point)
$1,500–$2,000 / 30 days
─────────────────────────────
This is how most of our client relationships start.
Full audit + strategic roadmap + 2–3 weeks of live execution.
No contract. No pressure. You see the work, we understand your business.

     ↓ converts to one of three retainers ↓

TIER 1 — SIGNAL          TIER 2 — ENGINE           TIER 3 — PROGRAM
$2,000/month              $3,500/month              $5,000/month
─────────────────         ─────────────────         ─────────────────
Social presence           Content + SEO machine     Influencer program
2 channels                Full content strategy     5–10 creators/month
12 posts/month            4 SEO articles/month      Creator management
Content calendar          16 social posts/month     Campaign execution
Monthly report            CEO/founder ghostwriting  ROI measurement
1 strategy call           Monthly KPI report        Executive report
```

### Naming rationale
- **Signal** — they know signal vs noise. A social presence is a signal in the market.
- **Engine** — content as an engine for pipeline. Builders love engines.
- **Program** — influencer "program" is the industry term (Vanessa used it at Fireflies.ai).
- All three are simple, specific, non-generic. No "Basic / Pro / Enterprise."

### Add-ons (bolt-on to any tier)
| Add-on | Price |
|--------|-------|
| CEO/Founder LinkedIn ghostwriting | +$800/month |
| AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) audit | $1,500 one-time |
| Campaign landing page (Angel builds) | $1,500–$2,500 one-time |
| Influencer campaign measurement report | $500–$1,000/month |

### Packaging rules (validated by research)
- Lead with **outcomes** in every tier description, not deliverables
- Highlight Tier 2 (Engine) as "Most Popular" — middle option wins by default
- Make the Pilot the easiest yes on the page — price it below what a client expects
- No custom quotes until they've talked to you — pre-qualify with public pricing first

## 4. Page-by-Page Structure

### HOME (/)

**Section 1 — Hero**
- Headline: Specific outcome + specific ICP (e.g. "Influencer programs and content that move pipeline. Built for B2B SaaS.")
- Subheadline: The differentiator ("Human-first. No AI slop. Marketing with ROI.")
- CTA (single): "Start with a 30-day pilot →"
- Supporting line: "No retainer commitment. No long contracts. Just results."

**Section 2 — Logo bar (trust transfer)**
- Archive / Fireflies.ai / ZENIQ
- Label: "Brands we've built programs for"

**Section 3 — Problem**
- "You're spending on content and influencers. But when you pull the metrics, the investment doesn't justify the return. If there's even any return."
- "You have a better product. You probably have more resources. Yet your competitor is getting all the engagement."
- "We fix that."
- 3 pain points, one sentence each: spend without ROI / better product invisible / competitor winning on noise not quality

**Section 4 — Services overview**
- 3 cards: Signal / Engine / Program
- Each card: icon, name, one-sentence outcome, "Learn more →"
- Not a full list — an orientation map

**Section 5 — How it works (3 steps)**
1. Start with a 30-day pilot
2. We learn your product, your ICP, your voice
3. We build the program. You own the results.

**Section 6 — Case study previews (2–3 cards)**
- Metric + company + campaign type
- "7 brands added to waitlist — Archive Radar Feature Launch"
- "Recurring revenue stream established — Archive AI Insider Newsletter"
- "2.45% engagement rate — Archive LinkedIn Content Strategy"
- CTA: "See all case studies →"

**Section 7 — Testimonials**
- 2–3 quotes with name, title, company, photo
- Specific outcomes in the quote, not generic praise

**Section 8 — Awards**
- Webby Awards — Best B2B Campaign
- Cannes Lions — Shortlist Recognition

**Section 9 — Pilot offer CTA block**
- "$1,500 for 30 days. Full audit, strategic roadmap, 2–3 weeks of live execution."
- CTA: "Book a pilot call →"
- Secondary: "See pricing →"

**Section 10 — FAQ (5–7 questions)**
- Do you work with early-stage startups?
- Do you run paid ads?
- How fast can we start?
- What if we don't have a creator budget?
- How is the pilot structured?
- What happens after the pilot?
- Do you work with DTC brands too?

**Section 11 — Footer**
- Repeat CTA: "Ready to build something real? →"
- Links: Services / Work / Pricing / About / Contact
- Social: LinkedIn, X (@vanessasemprun)
