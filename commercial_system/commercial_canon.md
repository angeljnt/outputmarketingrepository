# Commercial Canon

## Objective

Freeze one commercial system for Output Marketing and use it as the filter for:

- website copy
- service pages
- pricing
- outreach
- proposals
- CRM fields
- proof usage

## Core decision

Output Marketing should present a simpler public offer than its full internal delivery capacity.

That keeps the agency easier to buy, easier to explain, and easier to scale operationally.

## Offer architecture

### Core public offer

- `30-Day Pilot`
- `Foundation`
- `Engine`
- `Full Stack`

### Secondary custom offers

- `White-Label / Capacity Track`
- `Project Track`

### White-label rule

White-label is worth offering publicly, but not as a core homepage or pricing-grid package.

The right posture is:

- publicly discoverable
- clearly real
- positioned as custom
- secondary to the B2B SaaS growth offer

That means white-label can appear on:

- pricing FAQ
- contact page
- outreach docs
- about page
- custom-scope conversations

It should not lead:

- homepage hero
- primary pricing grid
- main narrative of the site

## Positioning

### Primary category

Output Marketing is a B2B SaaS content and organic growth agency.

### Core promise

We help founders and lean marketing teams turn founder voice, company content, SEO, and measurement into organic pipeline.

### Entry-point rule

Every qualified account should start through the `30-Day Pilot`, even when the likely end state is already obvious.

The pilot lowers buying risk, creates diagnostic clarity, and protects against forcing the wrong retainer.

## Active ICP

### Core ICP

- B2B SaaS
- AI SaaS
- revenue, sales, and customer-facing software
- workflow, ops, and automation software
- analytics, data, infrastructure, security, and compliance software

### Secondary ICP

- boutique agencies that need white-label execution capacity
- B2B service businesses with visible content or demand-gen motion
- education or EdTech companies with strong subject-matter content motion
- selected adjacent companies only when the need is clearly content, SEO, launches, reporting, or execution support

### Out of scope by default

- influencer-first work
- paid media as the main need
- video production as the main need
- PR-led scopes
- enterprise teams with large in-house execution capacity
- very early startups with no traction or no visible marketing motion
- staffing, recruiting, media, newsletter, and community-led businesses

## Product rules

### Packages

- Core public packages stay stable.
- Secondary custom offers stay real, but should not multiply into new public tiers.
- Add-ons expand the system without bloating the public package architecture.

### Services

- Services are organized by buying logic, not by isolated deliverables.
- No service family becomes public by default just because it exists internally.

### Proof

- Never claim a historical account literally bought today's packages unless that is true.
- Translate old work into capability proof, then into package relevance.

## Language rule

English is the only operating language for active product and marketing documents.
