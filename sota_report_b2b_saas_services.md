# SOTA Report: Marketing Services for B2B SaaS Companies and Founders
Date: 2026-03-24
Scope: What marketing services B2B SaaS companies (seed to Series B) and founders actually need — and what a 3-person boutique agency can own at a premium.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- Founder LinkedIn ghostwriting is the single highest-ROI, lowest-CAC service for B2B SaaS. One case study (Pylon): 1.2M impressions, 5.5x revenue growth, LinkedIn = ~1/3 of total pipeline. This is reproducible. Most SaaS companies are not doing it.
- 90% of SaaS companies post only from their company page. Employee/founder content gets 8x more engagement. This gap is the commercial opportunity.
- SEO + content delivers 702% ROI for B2B SaaS, breaks even in 7 months, and CAC drops from $480-942 to ~$290 as content compounds — vs $802 for paid search.
- Bottom-of-funnel content converts 10x-25x more than top-of-funnel. Most agencies sell traffic. The right agency sells pipeline.
- 47% of B2B marketers cannot prove content ROI. Agencies that include attribution dashboards retain clients. Agencies that don't get cut when budgets tighten.
- GEO (Generative Engine Optimization — getting cited in ChatGPT, Perplexity) converts 6x higher than Google. Early movers build moats. This is the emerging add-on.

## 2. What B2B SaaS Founders Actually Need (by stage)

### Pre-seed / Seed
At this stage, marketing is founder sales. The right service is not content volume — it's positioning.

| Need | What it means in practice |
|------|--------------------------|
| Language-market fit | Validate messaging with real prospects before publishing anything |
| ICP sharpening | Define the exact buyer — not everyone who could use the product |
| Sales enablement | 1-3 content pieces that move deals forward, not brand awareness |
| Founder LinkedIn presence | Start building before you have budget for ads — costs nothing but time |

Output Marketing role at this stage: positioning workshop + founder LinkedIn setup + 1 flagship content asset. Not a full retainer.

### Series A
Product-market fit is found. The goal is proving repeatable acquisition before Series B.

| Need | What it means in practice |
|------|--------------------------|
| Founder brand at scale | Ghostwritten LinkedIn cadence: 3x/week, consistent voice, built pipeline |
| Content engine | 4-8 SEO articles/month targeting revenue-intent terms (not informational fluff) |
| Attribution setup | GA4 + CRM integration to prove content drives demos and pipeline |
| Original research | One data study or report per quarter = 67% more top-10 rankings + AI citations |

Output Marketing role at this stage: Engine or Full Stack retainer. This is the core ICP.

### Series B
Proven traction. Marketing is now a growth lever, not an experiment.

| Need | What it means in practice |
|------|--------------------------|
| Thought leadership at scale | Founder brand + employee advocacy program |
| SEO/GEO integration | Rank for revenue terms AND get cited in AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity) |
| Distribution layer | Content repurposed across LinkedIn, email, and podcast |
| pSEO for product surface | Programmatic pages for integrations, use cases, comparisons |

Output Marketing role at this stage: Full Stack + pSEO project.

## 3. Refined Service Menu for B2B SaaS

### Core Services (retainer)

**1. Founder LinkedIn Ghostwriting**
- The highest-margin, hardest-to-commoditize service on the menu
- Voice matching + content calendar + 3 posts/week + carousel strategy
- Founder brief → ghostwriter draft → founder approval → publish
- Market rate: $2,000–$5,000/month per founder
- Output Marketing edge: Vanessa has done this — she IS the case study

**2. B2B Content Marketing (SEO + editorial)**
- Pain-Point SEO only: target terms where the searcher is already in buying mode
- 4-8 pieces/month depending on tier — journalist quality, not content mill
- Every piece: original perspective + expert insight + SEO intent alignment
- Include one original data asset per quarter (survey, benchmark, or analysis)
- Market rate: $2,000–$4,500/month
- Output Marketing edge: SaaS domain expertise means faster briefing, fewer revisions

**3. LinkedIn Brand Page Strategy**
- Company page cadence: 3-4 posts/week with carousels, case study snippets, product updates
- Pairs with founder ghostwriting — the brand page amplifies what the founder starts
- Includes Thought Leader Ads setup (amplify top-performing personal posts)
- Market rate: $1,200–$2,500/month standalone; bundled with ghostwriting

**4. Organic SEO**
- Technical audit + keyword strategy + on-page optimization
- Pair with content — never sell standalone
- Market rate: $1,500–$2,500/month

### Project Services

**5. pSEO Setup**
- Programmatic SEO: template + structured data = hundreds of ranking pages
- Best for: comparison pages, integration pages, use-case pages, alternatives pages
- Phase 1 (4-6 weeks): keyword cluster research + template build + CMS integration
- Fee: $4,000–$12,000 one-time
- Phase 2: maintenance retainer $1,500–$3,000/month

**6. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — add-on**
- Get client cited in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews
- Tactic: original research + structured data + citations from authoritative sources
- Execution: overlaps with content marketing — 20-30% add-on fee, not a separate retainer
- Market rate: $800–$2,000/month as an add-on

**7. One-time audits**
- Content audit: $1,500–$2,500
- SEO technical audit: $1,500–$3,000
- Positioning + messaging audit: $2,000–$4,000 (new addition — highest upstream value)

## 5. The Differentiation: What Makes Output Marketing Different From Generic Content Agencies

### 1. SaaS domain expertise
Generic agencies hire generalist writers. B2B SaaS buyers smell generic content instantly. A writer who understands churn, ARR, ICP, product-led growth, and the SaaS sales cycle produces content that earns the prospect's trust. This is what Animalz and Campfire charge premium for. It's what Output Marketing has.

### 2. Founder voice first
Most agencies produce company content. Output Marketing produces founder content — and the data shows founder content gets 8x more engagement than brand page content. Vanessa has built pipeline from personal brand. This is not theoretical.

### 3. Revenue attribution included
47% of B2B marketers can't prove content ROI. Output Marketing includes attribution dashboards in every tier. This turns content from a cost center into a defensible line item that survives budget cuts.

### 4. Positioned to serve seed-Series B (not enterprise)
The big agencies (Omniscient, Animalz) target $5M+ ARR companies. The generic mills target anyone. Output Marketing owns the gap: $300K–$5M ARR B2B SaaS, founder-led, Series A-ready or recently funded.

## 7. The Argument for Starting With Founders (not companies)

The highest-retention engagement pattern for B2B SaaS marketing agencies:
1. Land with founder ghostwriting (personal, high trust, high switching cost)
2. Add content marketing (extends the founder's perspective across search)
3. Add distribution layer (email + LinkedIn brand page = same content, more surface)
4. Add pSEO (product expansion, programmatic leverage)

Starting with the founder is cheaper to acquire, more defensible to retain, and naturally expands. A founder who trusts you with their voice doesn't fire you in month 3.

## 9. Anti-Patterns to Avoid When Serving B2B SaaS Clients

| Anti-pattern | Why it fails |
|-------------|-------------|
| Starting with tactics before positioning | Content about the wrong problem for the wrong buyer = noise |
| Prioritizing traffic over pipeline | Top-of-funnel content converts 10x-25x less than bottom-of-funnel |
| Generic AI-generated articles | Google penalizes thin content; SaaS buyers recognize generic output instantly |
| Only posting from company page | 8x less engagement than personal profile — confirmed by LinkedIn 2025 data |
| Month-to-month contracts | Content compounds over 6-12 months — short contracts kill results |
| No attribution | 47% of agencies don't track content ROI — clients who can't see it will cut it |
| Serving companies below $300K ARR as full retainer clients | Can't sustain retainer; high churn risk |
