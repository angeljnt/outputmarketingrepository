## Priority 0 — Vanessa's Existing Network (Start Here, Day 1)

Before any outreach strategy, cold emails, or LinkedIn sequences — the fastest path to a first client is someone who has already seen Vanessa's work.

**Who to contact immediately:**
- Former colleagues and leadership from **Archive** (Marketing Manager, 1.5 years)
- Former colleagues and leadership from **Fireflies.ai** (Influencer Marketing Manager, 4 months — a real B2B SaaS product)
- Brands and founders who reached out to Vanessa through **Creatorbuzz** for creator campaigns
- Former colleagues from **ZENIQ_Corp** (Marketing Manager, 2+ years, Dubai)
- LinkedIn connections who regularly engaged with Vanessa's content

**Why this works:** These people have seen the output. The trust is already there. A warm message asking if they know anyone who needs this — or if they want a 20-minute call — has a dramatically higher close rate than any cold outreach.

**The message (adapt to relationship):**
> "Hey [Name], hope you're doing well. I just launched my own marketing agency after my time at [Company]. We're working with B2B SaaS startups and DTC brands on influencer marketing, content, and social — the human kind, not the AI-generated stuff everyone's tired of. Do you know anyone who might be a fit? Or would you be open to a quick call to see if it makes sense for [their company]? Either way, no pressure."

**Goal:** 5–10 discovery calls booked in the first week from this channel alone.

## Client Profile A — B2B SaaS, Early-Stage (Primary)

**The ideal client:**
- US or Canada-based (California, New York, Toronto, Vancouver)
- Seed to Series A ($300K–$5M ARR)
- 5–40 employees
- No dedicated Marketing Manager — maybe a founder doing it themselves, or a Growth lead stretched too thin
- Has a product that works. Doesn't have a voice that lands.
- Has tried posting on LinkedIn inconsistently and it went nowhere
- Has heard about influencer marketing for SaaS but has no idea how to start

**Budget:** $2,000–$5,000/month

**Pain points:**
- Pipeline is too dependent on founder-led sales
- Content is sporadic and sounds like a product manual
- They know they need marketing but can't justify a $120K/year full-time hire yet
- They've been "about to work on content" for six months

**Service fit:**
- Entry point: Pilot + Social Presence or Content Engine
- Natural upsell: Influencer Launch once trust is established

**Where to find them:**
- LinkedIn: search "Founder" OR "CEO" AND "SaaS" → filter by company size 1–50 and location (California, New York, Ontario, BC)
- LinkedIn: search "Head of Growth" OR "Head of Marketing" → same filters
- ProductHunt: SaaS products that launched recently with low social traction
- Crunchbase: seed-stage B2B SaaS companies funded in the last 12 months
- Twitter/X: SaaS founders who post about building in public
- Slack communities: Indie Hackers, SaaS community Slacks, Lenny's Slack
- Reddit: r/SaaS, r/startups — founders asking marketing questions

**Trigger signals (reach out when you see these):**
- Just announced a funding round (especially seed or Series A)
- Just hired their first sales rep (they need marketing to match)
- Posted something on LinkedIn that got low engagement despite a good product
- Mentioned in a ProductHunt launch with few comments
- Their LinkedIn company page hasn't posted in 30+ days

## Client Profile B — DTC eCommerce with Traction (Secondary)

**The ideal client:**
- US or Canada-based (California especially — LA is a DTC hub)
- $500K–$5M in annual revenue
- Has a product that sells. Needs to scale without burning more on Meta Ads.
- Has tried paid social — it's working but it's getting expensive and unpredictable
- Hasn't built an influencer or creator strategy yet
- Has a founder or small team doing everything

**Budget:** $2,000–$4,000/month

**Pain points:**
- Meta and TikTok ad costs are eating margin
- They know micro-influencers work but don't know where to start
- Content feels like an afterthought — product photos and discount codes
- No email list strategy, no organic social momentum

**Service fit:**
- Entry point: Pilot + Social Presence
- Natural upsell: Influencer Launch (this is where the real revenue growth is for DTC)

**Where to find them:**
- Instagram: search by product category → look for brands with 5K–100K followers, consistent product posts, but low engagement on non-ad content
- TikTok Shop: brands selling with Shop but not leveraging creator partnerships properly
- LinkedIn: DTC founders often post about their brand journey
- Shopify Partner ecosystem / Shopify Plus community
- Reddit: r/ecommerce, r/Entrepreneur
- Twitter/X: DTC founder accounts (search "$X revenue" "bootstrapped" "ecommerce")
- LA-specific: LA startup scene, fashion/beauty/wellness brands

**Trigger signals:**
- Running visible Meta ads but low organic engagement
- Have a press mention or product review but no influencer strategy
- Recently crossed a revenue milestone and posted about it
- Posting product photos with low interaction (the content isn't the problem — the distribution is)

## Client Profile C — SaaS Company That Just Hired a Marketing Lead (Strategic)

**Why this is interesting:**
A company that just made their first marketing hire often immediately realizes they're under-resourced. A new Head of Marketing at a 20-person SaaS company has too much to do and not enough hands. They become a champion for outsourcing specific functions — especially ones like influencer and content where they know they need expertise they don't have yet.

**Where to find them:**
- LinkedIn job change notifications (if you're connected to them)
- LinkedIn search: "new" AND "Head of Marketing" OR "VP Marketing" → filter by SaaS companies, 10–100 employees

**The pitch to this person:**
Don't pitch to compete with them. Pitch to support them. "You're building the team. We can be your influencer and content arm while you focus on strategy and demand gen." They become an internal champion, not a replaced job.

## The Pilot as the Door Opener

For any profile above, the answer to hesitation is always the pilot:

> "Before you commit to anything, let's do 30 days together. You see the work. We understand your business. $1,500–$2,000. At the end of the month, you decide. No contracts, no pressure."

A client who signs a $1,500 pilot and sees results will convert to a $3,500–$5,000 retainer. One pilot to one retainer = $42,000–$60,000/year in revenue from a single client relationship.
