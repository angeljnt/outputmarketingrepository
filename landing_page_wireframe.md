# Output Marketing — Homepage Wireframe
Research basis: SOTA findings (5 pillars), no testimonials constraint
Framework: PASO (Problem → Agitate → Solution → Offer) + Stacked Proof
Date: 2026-03-22

---

## Research Decisions Applied

| Decision | Source | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| PASO scroll sequence | CXL, Unbounce, Framer 2025 | Validated psychological order for B2B services |
| 3 CTA placements (hero, post-proof, footer) | Multiple A/B studies | Captures ready, convinced, and thorough buyers |
| Logos immediately below hero | comScore: +69% conversion | Highest efficiency trust signal, lowest cognitive load |
| Awards after problem, before services | CRO practitioners 2025 | Bridges skepticism before pitch begins |
| Damaging admission after services | Cleveland State study + Cialdini | Increases credibility of remaining claims |
| No testimonial placeholder | Anti-pattern confirmed | Worse than no social proof at all |
| Specific numbers throughout | Unbounce, CXL consensus | "2.45%" beats "high engagement" every time |
| FAQ as inline objection handling | 80% conversion lift data | Not a glossary — a sales script |

## Anti-Patterns Explicitly Avoided

| Anti-pattern | Why avoided |
|--------------|-------------|
| "Testimonials coming soon" | Worse than no social proof |
| Multiple competing CTAs | 266% conversion drop |
| Generic hero ("full-service agency") | Immediate bounce |
| Feature lists in service cards | Outcomes convert, features don't |
| FAQ only at bottom | Inline objection handling = 80% lift |
| Awards mentioned in text only | Official badges required — visual beats text |
| Stock photography | Founder photo converts better |
| "Book a call" generic CTA | Specific beats generic ("30-day pilot") |
| AI-sounding copy | Reduces trust, detectable by buyers |

## Copy Rules Applied Throughout

Per brand_voice.md:
- One idea per sentence
- Short paragraphs — max 3 sentences
- No em dashes
- No AI slop words: tapestry, landscape, robust, seamless, transformative, leverage, unlock
- Numbers are specific — "2.45%" not "high"
- ICP language used naturally: MRR, ARR, CAC, ICP, pipeline, PMF
- Damaging admission delivered straight — no hedging
- "We fix that" — direct, confident, no explanation
