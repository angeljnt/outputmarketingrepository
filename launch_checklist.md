# Agency Launch Checklist

---

## Foundation

- [ ] Choose and register the agency name
- [ ] Set up a professional email (hello@[agency].com or vanessa@[agency].com)
- [ ] Angel builds the landing page (1 page: tagline, 3 packages, contact CTA)
- [ ] Set up Stripe and/or Wise for USD payments from US/CA clients
- [ ] Set up a simple CRM or tracking spreadsheet (prospect → contacted → call booked → proposal sent → closed)

---

## Vanessa's LinkedIn (Highest ROI action before any outreach)

- [ ] Update headline to reflect the agency ("Founder @ [Agency] | B2B Marketing, But Make It Human | Influencer & Content Strategy for SaaS")
- [ ] Update About section to speak to ideal clients (B2B SaaS founders, DTC brands in the US/CA)
- [ ] Add the agency as current experience
- [ ] Pin a post or featured item that showcases a result or a point of view

---

## Warm Network Outreach (Start Day 1 — Highest Priority)

- [ ] Build a list of 30–50 contacts from Vanessa's network: Archive, Fireflies.ai, Creatorbuzz, ZENIQ, LinkedIn connections who engaged with her content
- [ ] Send personalized message to each contact (not a mass blast — one by one)
- [ ] Follow up with anyone who didn't respond after 4–5 days
- [ ] Track responses and book discovery calls
- [ ] Goal: 5–10 discovery calls booked from warm network in first week

---

## LinkedIn Cold Outreach (Week 2 onward)

- [ ] Define the 2 primary ICP search queries on LinkedIn (e.g., "Founder" + "SaaS" + California + <50 employees)
- [ ] Build a list of 100 prospects
- [ ] Start engagement sequence: like/comment on their content (Day 1 of sequence)
- [ ] Send personalized connection request (Day 3 of sequence)
- [ ] Send short, specific message after connection accepted (Day 5 of sequence)
- [ ] Set up follow-up cadence (2–5 days between touches, max 3 touches)
- [ ] Goal: 20–30 outreach sequences running per week

---

## Discovery Call Process

- [ ] Set up a Calendly or Cal.com link for 30-minute discovery calls
- [ ] Prepare 5 questions to ask on every discovery call:
  - What does your marketing look like today?
  - What have you tried that didn't work?
  - Where do you most need traction — awareness, leads, credibility?
  - What's your budget for this?
  - What would a win look like in 60 days?
- [ ] At end of call: always present the pilot offer first ($1,500–$2,000/30 days)
- [ ] Follow up within 24 hours with a brief summary and next step

---

## Proposal & Closing

- [ ] Create a 1-page proposal template (problem → solution → deliverables → price → terms)
- [ ] Include the pilot offer as the default entry point for new clients
- [ ] Send proposals within 24 hours of a positive discovery call
- [ ] Follow up 3 days after proposal if no response, with a piece of relevant insight (not "just checking in")
- [ ] Collect signed agreement before starting work (even for pilots — a simple 1-page contract)

---

## Content & Credibility (Vanessa posts on LinkedIn)

- [ ] Write and publish: "Why I left [X] to start my own agency" (personal story, resonates with founders)
- [ ] Write and publish: "The problem with B2B marketing in 2026 that no one talks about" (thought leadership)
- [ ] Write and publish: "What we do differently" (specific, not generic)
- [ ] Write and publish: "Why we're only a handful of people — and why that's a feature, not a bug"
- [ ] Engage daily with content from target ICPs (SaaS founders, DTC brand owners in CA/NY/Toronto)

---

## First Client Onboarding

- [ ] Collect payment before starting work
- [ ] Send a simple welcome email outlining: what to expect, how you communicate, what you need from them
- [ ] Schedule a kickoff call (30–45 min) to align on goals and brand voice
- [ ] Deliver first output within the first 7 days of the pilot — early momentum matters
- [ ] Check in at the 2-week mark with a brief progress update (don't wait for the end of month)

---

## Month-End Checkpoints

- [ ] Send every client a simple monthly report (results vs. goals, what's working, what's next)
- [ ] Ask every pilot client for a testimonial or a LinkedIn recommendation at the end of month 1
- [ ] Formally offer the retainer conversion before the pilot ends — don't wait for them to bring it up
- [ ] Ask every happy client: "Do you know anyone else who could use this?"
