## 5. pSEO Viability Analysis

### What it is
Programmatic SEO = template-driven system that generates hundreds to thousands of SEO-optimized landing pages from structured data. Instead of writing each page manually, you build a template once and populate it with a dataset.

### Evidence it works for B2B SaaS
- UserPilot: 25K → 100K monthly organic visitors in 10 months
- Storylane: +201% traffic, 400+ demo requests, 140+ paying customers in 6 months
- CommandBar: 340% organic lead growth, no in-house SEO expert
- Generic case (omnius.so): 67 → 2,100 monthly signups from pSEO alone

### Most effective B2B SaaS page types for pSEO
1. Comparison pages: "[Tool A] vs [Tool B]" — high buyer intent, easy to template
2. Integration pages: "[Product] + [Integration]" — Zapier's entire strategy
3. Use-case pages: "[Product] for [Industry/Role]"
4. Alternative pages: "Best [Tool] alternatives"
5. Template/resource pages: "[Type] template for [use case]"

### The risk Kevin Indig flags (and how to mitigate)
Risk: Google's Helpful Content Update (2024) penalizes thin, purely templated pages. Google has called low-quality pSEO "spam."
Mitigation: Each page must have original data, not just template fill. Add client-specific data, unique metrics, or curated comparisons to each page cluster. Human/AI hybrid is the right approach — AI generates the scaffolding, a human adds the original insight layer.

### Delivery model for Output Marketing
- **Phase 1 (project, 4–6 weeks):** Keyword cluster research → page type selection → template build → data source setup → CMS integration → QA. Fee: $4,000–$12,000 depending on scale.
- **Phase 2 (retainer):** Monthly page additions, performance monitoring, content layer updates. Fee: $1,500–$3,000/month.
- **Tools required (no-code viable):** Webflow + Whalesynch (simplest), or Airtable + any headless CMS. Angel's technical background makes this feasible without hiring a developer.
- **Client fit:** B2B SaaS companies with a definable integration ecosystem or clear use-case matrix. Not for clients who sell to everyone.

### Verdict
**Yes — offer pSEO. Offer it as a standalone project product, not folded into a monthly retainer.** The setup fee model generates high-margin one-time revenue. The maintenance retainer generates recurring revenue. The work is repeatable once you build your first template system. The niche is not saturated at the boutique level.

| Anti-pattern | Why it kills you |
|-------------|------------------|

## 7. What the Evidence Says About Service Combinations

The highest-retention boutique agencies share this pattern:
- **Anchor service** with strong recurring revenue (content or LinkedIn)
- **Complementary service** that uses the same research/inputs (SEO + content)
- **Premium add-on** that signals sophistication (pSEO, founder ghostwriting)
- **Measurement layer** included in every tier (KPI report, dashboard)

Campfire Labs (near 100% renewal rate): journalist-quality content + SaaS domain expertise + consistent cadence.
Animalz model: thought leadership + SEO for growth-stage SaaS = premium pricing, premium retention.

Both run lean. Both say no to most service requests. Both charge more than the market average.
