# Clay Outreach Process

## Purpose

This document captures the working process used to build a Clay outreach list for Output Marketing based on the agency's current ICP, services, and positioning.

The goal is not to build the biggest list possible. The goal is to build a smaller, sharper list of companies that are actually likely to hire an agency for:

- Content marketing
- SEO content
- Social and LinkedIn execution
- Product and launch marketing
- Marketing ops and reporting

## Agency Positioning

Output Marketing is being positioned as an agency, not as a freelancer or single operator.

The strongest offers for outreach are:

- Content marketing
- SEO content
- Social media execution
- LinkedIn support
- Product and launch marketing
- Webinar and campaign support
- Marketing ops and reporting
- White-label support for agencies
- Creative support for social and email

Influencer marketing is not a priority service in this workflow.

## Geographic Focus

Primary target markets:

- United States
- Canada
- UAE

For the sharper 80/20 outreach pass, the list was narrowed mostly to:

- California
- Texas
- New York
- Massachusetts

## Best-Fit ICP

The strongest-fit companies are:

- Growth-stage B2B SaaS companies
- AI SaaS companies
- Revenue, sales, and customer-facing software companies
- Workflow, ops, and automation software companies
- Analytics, data, and technical infrastructure software companies

Lower priority:

- Boutique agencies needing white-label support
- B2B service businesses with strong marketing motion
- Selected DTC brands only when the fit is clearly content and social execution

## Clay Search Logic

### First pass: company search

Start with `Find companies`, not `Find people`.

Reason:

- It is easier to control company fit first
- It reduces wasted enrichment later
- It keeps the contact search focused on real targets

### Core company filters

Use these broad starting filters:

- Company type:
  - Privately held
- Size:
  - 11 to 50 employees
  - 51 to 200 employees if available
- Geography:
  - United States
  - Canada
  - UAE

### Industries to include

Use the strongest software-heavy categories available:

- Software Development
- Technology, Information and Internet
- IT Services and IT Consulting

Use cautiously or deprioritize:

- Advertising Services
- Business Consulting and Services
- Internet Publishing
- E-Learning Providers
- Market Research

## Clay Workflow

### Step 1: run broad search

Run the company search with the filters above and save to a new table.

Do not enrich contacts yet.

### Step 2: create the company table

Save results to a new table with a clear name, for example:

- `Target Companies`
- `ICP Companies`
- `B2B SaaS Agency Targets`

### Step 3: dedupe

Deduplicate by `Name` first.

If Clay allows it later, dedupe by:

- Name
- Domain

### Step 4: clean the list

Before finding contacts, clean the table by:

- removing duplicate companies
- filtering out tiny weak-fit companies
- excluding obvious media, recruiting, and consulting businesses
- keeping software-heavy categories

### Step 5: narrow geography

For the strongest 80/20 pass, prioritize:

- California
- Texas
- New York
- Massachusetts

### Step 6: create fit scoring

Add a `Company Fit` select column with:

- High
- Medium
- Low
- Exclude

Use it before enriching contacts.

### Step 7: only enrich the best companies

Do not enrich all source rows.

Only enrich:

- High-fit companies
- possibly Medium-fit companies in a second wave

Ignore:

- Low
- Exclude

## Company Fit Rules

### High

Mark `High` when a company:

- is a software or platform company
- is in CA, TX, NY, or MA
- sounds B2B
- has product, platform, automation, analytics, revenue, sales, customer, security, compliance, or workflow language
- looks like it could benefit from content, launches, social, or reporting support

### Medium

Mark `Medium` when a company:

- is real software, but the buying need is less obvious
- looks potentially valuable, but not urgent
- may fit better in a second wave

### Low

Mark `Low` when a company:

- technically fits the category
- but has weak GTM signals
- or unclear need for agency support

### Exclude

Mark `Exclude` when a company is:

- a consulting shop
- a recruiting or talent business
- a newsletter, media brand, or community
- an agency
- a vague stealth company
- an acquired or stale company not worth prioritizing

## Top 20% Logic

The final source pool should not be used as a full outreach list.

Instead, use it as a source database and narrow to the top 20% most likely buyers.

In this workflow, the top 20% means:

- software-heavy companies
- product-led companies
- companies in California, Texas, New York, and Massachusetts
- companies with strong B2B product language
- companies without obvious service-business or media signals

This top slice is the first contact enrichment pool.

## Practical Recommendation

Do not start outreach from the full raw export.

Use this order:

1. Build company source list
2. Dedupe
3. Clean and filter
4. Score `Company Fit`
5. Narrow to top 20%
6. Enrich contacts only for the best companies
7. Start outreach with the first-wave targets

## First-Wave Focus

The first outreach wave should concentrate on companies most likely to buy:

- Content marketing
- SEO content
- Social and LinkedIn execution
- Product and launch marketing
- Marketing ops and reporting

This first wave should be much smaller than the total source pool and should emphasize quality over volume.
