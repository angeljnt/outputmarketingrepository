# ICP Rundown: Agency Targets

## Overview

This document breaks down the strongest ICP segments from the final Clay output based on Output Marketing's current agency scope:

- Content marketing
- SEO content
- Social and LinkedIn execution
- Product and launch marketing
- Marketing ops and reporting
- White-label support for lean teams

The goal is to focus on the companies most likely to hire an agency for execution support, not just any company we could theoretically help.

## Core ICP

### 1. B2B SaaS and AI SaaS

This is the strongest category overall.

Why they fit:

- Lean teams with real growth pressure
- Clear need for content and distribution
- Frequent product updates, launches, and demos
- Strong fit for content, SEO, social, and launch support

Best services to pitch:

- Content marketing
- SEO content
- Product and launch marketing
- Social and LinkedIn execution
- Marketing ops and reporting

Examples:

- GIS Cloud
- Mage
- Gumloop
- Hatica
- Anyword
- Etleap
- Datatron
- Cypris
- Cleanlab
- Reflex
- Peakflo
- Thread

### 2. Revenue, Sales, and Customer-Facing Software

This is one of the best commercial sub-segments because the value proposition is easier to tie to pipeline and customer acquisition.

Why they fit:

- They already think in GTM terms
- Easier to connect content and campaigns to revenue
- More likely to care about launches, demos, and follow-through content

Best services to pitch:

- Content marketing
- SEO content
- LinkedIn and social execution
- Webinar and launch support
- Reporting support

Examples:

- Emitrr
- Sequence
- MonetizeNow
- Whippy
- Kasisto
- CaliberMind
- Gleantap

### 3. Workflow, Ops, and Automation Software

This is a strong fit when the product is technical and the team needs help translating product value into market-facing execution.

Why they fit:

- Technical products often need clearer messaging
- Launches and feature rollouts need better support
- Lean teams benefit from an external execution layer

Best services to pitch:

- Product and launch marketing
- Content marketing
- SEO content
- Reporting and ops support

Examples:

- Tuebora
- CloudQuery
- Project Insight
- Flexxbotics
- Pliant
- Vivantio

### 4. Analytics, Data, and Technical Infrastructure Software

Good fit when the company already has market motion and needs better positioning, content, and campaign support.

Why they fit:

- Technical products often need stronger market translation
- Good match for product-led SEO and thought-leadership content
- Clear fit for content, launches, and LinkedIn support

Best services to pitch:

- Content marketing
- SEO content
- Product marketing
- LinkedIn support
- Reporting support

Examples:

- Novi Labs
- Datatron
- QC Ware
- MergerWare
- CausalFunnel
- Evervault

## Priority Breakdown

### First Wave

These should be the first outreach focus:

- B2B SaaS
- AI SaaS
- Revenue and GTM software
- Workflow and automation tools
- Analytics and data platforms

### Second Wave

These are still worth keeping, but behind the first group:

- More technical infrastructure software
- Vertical SaaS with clear product motion
- Less obvious but still credible B2B software companies

### Downrank or Exclude

These should not be the focus even if they appear in the source pool:

- Consulting-led IT firms
- Dev shops and implementation-heavy firms
- Staffing, recruiting, or talent-led businesses
- Community, media, or newsletter companies
- Vague stealth companies
- Acquired or stale companies that do not look active

## Practical Use

Use the final Clay pool as a source database, but only enrich and prioritize the highest-fit companies first.

Recommended working structure:

- High: clear B2B software fit with strong product and GTM signals
- Medium: software fit is real, but demand or execution need is less obvious
- Low: technically fits, but weak signal or unclear buying motion
- Exclude: service-heavy, media-heavy, staffing-heavy, or unclear companies

## Suggested Next Step

Build a first-wave list of 250 to 400 companies from the highest-fit slice above, then enrich contacts only for those companies.
